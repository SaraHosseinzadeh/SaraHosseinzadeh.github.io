About Me

My Story

I am an Industrial Engineer with a strong academic foundation in Operations Research, focusing on analytical modeling and structured decision-making in complex systems. My work centers on developing rigorous approaches for Optimization under Uncertainty, designing mathematical models that address real-world challenges in dynamic and uncertain environments.

During my M.Sc., I developed multi-objective optimization models for energy planning, contributing to research in Sustainable Systems by integrating economic and environmental considerations into long-term infrastructure decisions. I have also worked on modeling problems in Supply Chain Systems, particularly in agile scheduling and system performance improvement.

Alongside my optimization background, I am increasingly interested in Decision Analytics and Data-Driven Modeling, exploring how data-informed insights can enhance traditional analytical frameworks and support more adaptive and intelligent decision systems.

My goal is to pursue doctoral research that advances methodological rigor in Operations Research while contributing to impactful applications in sustainable and complex industrial systems.
